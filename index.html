Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

As you navigate through the multiple charts available we can observe some interesting facts between
the lack of healtcare, smoker's rate and obesity rate versus poverty rate, median age and median household income:

Let's first observe the poverty rate :
We observe a small positive correlation between the poverty rate and the lack a healthcare in the different regions. This means the higher the poverty rate in a region the bigger the lack of healthcare.
The observation is the same between the smoker's rate and the poverty rate. This correlation seems a bit stronger than the previous one. The higher the poverty rate in a region, the higher the smoker's rate.
We observe the same trend between obesity and poverty rate. The higher the poverty rate the higher the obesity rate. This is the strongest positive correlation observed for the poverty rate.

Let's now observe the median age :
We observe a small negative correlation between the median age and the lack of healthcare. This means that the younger, the less subscriptions to a healthcare are observed in a region.
The correlation is less visible between the median age and the percent smokers in a region. A small positive correlation
The median age doesn't explain the obesity rate.No correlation visible

Let's finally observe the household income :
We observe a small negative correlation between the median income and the lack of healthcare. This means that the less income, the less someone subscribes to a healthcare.
The negative correlation is much more visible between the median income and the percent smokers in a region.The less people earn, the more they smoke
We observe the same trend between the obesity and the income, the obesity rate is higher where the median income is lower
